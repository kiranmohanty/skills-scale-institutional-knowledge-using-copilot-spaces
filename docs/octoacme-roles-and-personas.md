# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Test Engineer

### Role Summary
QA/Test Engineers ensure quality standards are met by planning, executing, and automating tests. They validate that changes meet acceptance criteria before release and track defects to resolution.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain comprehensive test plans and test cases
- Perform manual and automated testing across features
- Validate bug fixes and execute regression test suites
- Identify and document defects with clear reproduction steps
- Communicate quality risks and testing blockers to the team
- Participate in release readiness assessments
- Maintain test infrastructure and automation frameworks

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and reliability
- Catch defects early in the development cycle
- Reduce production incidents and escalations
- Enable continuous delivery with confidence

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and defect reports
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Release readiness sign-offs
- Collaboration with developers on bug fixes and automation strategies

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Review acceptance criteria, discuss test cases, validate fixes
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- **With Release Managers**: Confirm release readiness and execute smoke tests
- **With Project Managers**: Report blockers and schedule testing activities

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers champion user experience by designing intuitive workflows, interfaces, and interactions. They ensure accessibility, usability, and design consistency across products.

### Responsibilities
- Develop wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and incorporate user feedback
- Maintain and evolve style guides and design systems
- Define interaction patterns and user workflows
- Ensure accessibility standards are met (WCAG compliance)
- Review implemented designs during development
- Collaborate on design documentation and specifications

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support burden
- Ensure consistent, accessible design across the product
- Validate design decisions through testing and metrics

### Typical Communication
- Design mockups, wireframes, and prototypes
- Design specifications and component documentation
- Usability testing results and insights
- Design review sessions with developers and product managers

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user needs and product vision
- **With Developers**: Review implementation against design specs, discuss technical constraints
- **With QA Engineers**: Define acceptance criteria for visual and interaction testing
- **With Business Analysts**: Understand user requirements and workflows

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze workflows, and facilitate understanding across business and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and prioritize business requirements
- Map current and future business processes
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Create requirements specifications and user stories
- Analyze data and propose process improvements
- Facilitate solution workshops and design reviews
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure business needs are clearly understood
- Reduce misalignment between business and technical teams
- Deliver solutions that address root causes, not just symptoms
- Enable informed decision-making through data and analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and specifications
- Process flow diagrams and decision matrices
- Stakeholder workshop notes and action items
- Business case analysis and ROI assessments

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Refine requirements and prioritization
- **With Developers**: Clarify technical feasibility and discuss trade-offs
- **With Project Managers**: Identify dependencies and scope changes
- **With Stakeholders**: Gather requirements and validate solutions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate the planning, coordination, and verification of releases. They manage deployment schedules, oversee cutover activities, and lead incident response when issues arise.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain release calendar and deployment schedules
- Coordinate cutover activities across teams (QA, DevOps, Support)
- Prepare and review release notes and communication plans
- Verify readiness criteria before deployments
- Execute rollback procedures when necessary
- Lead post-incident reviews and triage root causes
- Document lessons learned and process improvements
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver releases on time with minimal risk
- Reduce deployment incidents and production outages
- Enable rapid rollback when needed
- Build confidence in release processes

### Typical Communication
- Release plans and go/no-go decisions
- Deployment runbooks and rollback procedures
- Incident notifications and status updates
- Post-release retrospectives and action items

### Key Interactions
- **With QA Engineers**: Confirm readiness, coordinate smoke testing
- **With Developers**: Coordinate hotfixes and urgent deployments
- **With Project Managers**: Align on release schedule and dependencies
- **With DevOps/Infrastructure**: Manage deployment execution and monitoring
- **With Stakeholders**: Provide release updates and incident communications

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When defining project teams, explicitly assign these roles to clarify expectations and accountability.
